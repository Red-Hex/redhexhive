RSA Project - AI 100

This was an individual project which will later be submitted to the RSA Design Awards. I had the choice of 9 briefs picked brief 1 known as “AI 100”. A quick summary of the brief is how can we use Artificial Intelligence to help people live a happy, healthy 100-year life.

My Final Idea

A platform for users to get updates about their current health and how they can improve it. The data from their current health is collected using implantable microchips which will measure data when an RFID chip is scanned using the provided scanner, and the web app. Every user will have their own unique ID through the use of microchipping which will enable them to log into their account through scanning their chip on the login page of the web app.

Skills Required

  • Arduino
  • UI/UX Design (Adobe XD)
  • PHP
  • SQL

Time Taken: 12 weeks

Planning and reserach

For this project I had to write a case study as well as build a prototype for my solution so my research I did for the case study linked together with what I wanted to make for solution to this brief. After analysing the brief and highlighting the important facts of what my solution should look like, I began to research Microchipping and the uses it has in the medical industry followed by researching Artificial Intelligence and the impact it can have on the medical industry. From my research I learned that there were many ways that microchip technology was used throughout the medical industry and can measure a person’s pulse, which is what I wanted to my microchip to do but only when scanned on the web app.

Initial Ideas

For my initial brainstorm I had a few ideas about an AI companion of sorts that would look after your health, from this brainstorm I narrowed down to a web app companion or a physical robot companion. After considering the time I had to complete the project and what would be a more unique idea, I went ahead with the microchipping AI companion idea.

Development of Ideas

After fleshing out my idea more the solution would come down to three main components. A web app, a physical scanner and the implantable microchip.

Technologies

For the web app I used PHP to create the front end and structure of the website, as well as a login and sign up form, and I created an SQL database for the backend using phpMyAdmin. Using PHP allowed me to create a full working prototype that can be added to over time before submission to the RSA and would also allow me to test my solution in the real world by getting users to sign up and have heir data in the database

Security

From my initial primary research, it was very clear that data protection and security of one of the biggest concerns for my solution. My first idea was to encrypt the medical data but the problem with this was if a hacker was able to decrypt it, they would have access to everyone’s medical data.

Problems Faced

One of the main problems I faced in this project was getting more than one card authorised on my scanner. I overcame this by adding in an eslseif statment in order to have two IDs be authorised on the scanner at the same time. This is only a temproary fix but shows the use of multiple RFID tags at the same time. I also had an issue of getting the correct heart rate reading, to fix this I added in a simple equation to calculate the BPM and display the correct number.
